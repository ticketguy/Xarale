F.A.Q

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some of the questions people ask.

  • What Is Fiber Splicing?

    Splicing in optical fiber is the joining two fiber optic cables together. There are 2 methods of cable splicing, mechanical or fusion.

  • What is an Outside Plant? (OSP)

    Outside plant (OSP) is the installation of fiber optic cables outside the infastructural premises. These includes installing aerial cable, direct-buried cable, underground cable in conduit or installing conduit or innerduct and then pulling cable, or placing cable underwater, constructing manholes and splicing joints outside the premises.

  • Will fiber be the best solution for network connections in the future?

    Even though it’s not likely fiber will replace all of our connections any time soon, it’s a fascinating technology that can give your business an edge if it’s an option. What Is Fiber? Fiber is made up of single or multiple strands of glass, covered in a protective sheath. - forbes

  • What Is FTTH?

    Fiber to the home (FTTH), also called fiber to the premises (FTTP), is the installation and use of optical fiber from a central point directly to individual buildings such as residences, apartment buildings and businesses to provide high-speed internet access.

  • What does FTTT stand for?

    FTTT. Fiber to the Tower (fiber optics), this involves fiber connection to the tower through the patch panels.

  • What is the maximum amount of bandwidth a fiber cable can carry?

    This question is difficult. Usually, fiber cables carry an average of a few terabytes of data per second. Some can go up to hundreds of terabytes per second. You can theoretically send one pulse per femtosecond, or 0.000000000000001 seconds, or so.
